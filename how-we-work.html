How We Work

Our three-layer learning ecosystem, methodologies, and governance approaches

Our Learning Ecosystem: A Commons-Based Forest

We organize learning through three interconnected layers that function like a healthy forest ecosystem. Drawing from Elinor Ostrom's work on common resource pooling, each layer follows regenerative design principles where resources flow naturally between levels, creating abundance rather than scarcity.

Soil/Commons Layer - The Foundational Nutrients

Like rich forest soil that provides nutrients for all plant life, this layer contains our shared resources, wisdom, and relationships. Monthly community calls, open-source toolkits, our peer-to-peer podcast, and ongoing conversations create the fertile ground where everything else grows.

Knots Layer - Where Individual Learning Happens

As Raimón Panikkar said, "A person is a knot in a net of relationships." Like specific trees and plants in a forest that create distinct growing environments, these are our dojos and guilds where people develop particular skills while tending the relationships that constitute them.

Social Web Layer - Where Collective Impact Happens

Like the forest canopy where different species interact and create conditions for rain and air circulation, this is where individual learning translates into collective action through deep dive labs, organizational consulting, and cross-pollination between different learning communities.

Our Core Methodologies

We use proven approaches that honor emergence over engineering:

Open Space Technology

Large group meetings where the agenda emerges from participants' genuine passions. Based on four principles: "Whoever comes is the right people," "Whatever happens is the only thing that could have," "Whenever it starts is the right time," and "When it's over, it's over."

Art of Hosting

A collection of practices including circle processes, World Café, and appreciative inquiry that create conditions for collective intelligence to emerge. Focuses on hosting conversations that matter.

Circle Processes and Council Methods

Indigenous-rooted practices for decision-making and deep listening where each person has equal voice and opportunity to speak and listen.

Sociocracy

A governance system based on consent decision-making rather than consensus or hierarchy. Decisions proceed when there are no remaining paramount objections.

Warm Data Labs

Developed by Nora Bateson, these processes explore complex topics through multiple ways of knowing, focusing on relationships that integrate systems.

Gift Economy Principles

Economic relationships where giving creates and maintains relationships rather than ending them, based on dhana (Buddhist gift-giving) and needs-based accessibility.

How to Engage at Each Layer

Soil/Commons Layer - Start Here if You're Curious

Free access to foundational resources:

  • Monthly community calls facilitated with Art of Hosting practices
  • Peer-to-peer podcast featuring practitioners sharing real successes and failures
  • Open-source toolkit using Creative Commons licensing
  • Connection with practitioners across different fields

Knots Layer - Deepen Your Practice

Skill-building communities that honor both individual development and collective wisdom:

CommDojo

Monthly practice using T-group and Bohm dialogue for authentic communication.

Conflict Kinship Dojo

Monthly gatherings using restorative justice circles and nonviolent communication.

Learning Guilds

8-12 week intensive programs with mentorship and peer coaching.

Soul Space

Embodied fishbowl practice open to the public.

Social Web Layer - Collective Action and Deep Inquiry

Where individual learning translates into collective impact:

  • ATEM Labs: Alternatives to Extractive Models explorations using warm data methodology
  • Organizational Support: Consulting through CrowdWork cooperative that builds capacity
  • Transformative Services: Journey coaching, Work Tech/Power Design, custom retreats

Access and Pricing Philosophy

We operate with multiple levels of resourcing based on gift economy principles:

Our Approach to Money and Access:

  • Dhana (Gift Economy): Contribute what feels right based on your capacity
  • Needs-based Scholarships: No one turned away for lack of funds
  • Standard Pricing: For those with capacity to support others' scholarships
  • Work-trade: Contribute skills in exchange for participation
  • Organizational Sponsorship: Companies fund community member access

Governance and Decision-Making

We use sociocratic principles adapted for network organizing:

How Decisions Get Made

  • Consent-based: Decisions proceed when there are no remaining paramount objections
  • Circle structure: Different domains have autonomy within their areas
  • Community input: Major direction changes discussed in monthly calls
  • Conflict resolution: Using our own conflict navigation practices

Organizational Structure

Educational work is fiscally sponsored through Inquiring Systems Institute. Consulting work happens through CrowdWork cooperative. This separation allows us to maintain gift economy principles for learning while ensuring legal protection for professional services.
