Support · Made in the commons

This is
not a
product.

The Present of Work is produced in the commons. No ads. No sponsored content. No investors expecting a return. That's not an accident — it's the argument made material.

What that means

The economics
of it.

A publication that argues against the commodification of human beings should probably not be funded by commodifying its audience. So this project doesn't run ads, doesn't sell data, and doesn't take venture capital. The trade-off is obvious: it runs on contribution, not extraction.

There is no paywall. The publication is free to read. The podcast is free to listen. The knowledge map is free to explore. Everything produced here is a public good. If you find value in it, you can choose to support it. If you can't, that's fine too — come back and read.

1
Research and writing time. Each essay in the publication takes significant reading, thinking, and drafting. Contributors are paid for their work — because the argument that care work should be compensated applies here too.
2
Podcast production. Recording, editing, sound design, transcription — Field Notes is building a real production practice. That requires tools and time.
3
Infrastructure. Hosting, the knowledge map, the site. Built lean; still costs something.
4
Translation and accessibility. The goal is for this to be available in more than one language and genuinely accessible to people who use assistive technology.
5
The ecology itself. Some portion of support goes toward the broader web — other organisms in this archipelago that don't yet have their own funding.
