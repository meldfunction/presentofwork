Series I
The History
of the
Present
How did selling your time become the definition of a good life?
Not history for its own sake — genealogy. Each post traces how something we take for granted was constructed, and therefore could be constructed differently. From pre-wage economies through the Industrial Revolution to the 20th century office, this series asks what choices were made at each turn, and who made them.

Series II
The Future
of the
Present
Why do all our promised futures still need you to earn your place in them?
A reckoning with every technological utopia from the Jetsons to AGI — and what each one reveals about what we actually believe. The Jetsons, cyberspace, VR, the sharing economy, artificial general intelligence: each wave promised liberation. Each one reconfigured who captures the gains. This series asks what that pattern means.

The
Present
What if your worth was never up for negotiation?
Not what work will be. What we decide it should be — right now. This series is ever-growing: plural economies, practices of resistance, new structures, and what it means to act before the patterns lock in. No grand theory. Many experiments, honestly reported.
